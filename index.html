Warm up by practicing scales at a fast tempo. Then practice your excerpt at different tempos, starting at below tempo to eventually 20 bpm above tempo.

Turn on a metronome and practice scalse in 16th notes. Then practice your excerpt at below tempo and work up until you are above tempo

Practice single and double tounging eighth notes at a slow tempo. Then increase tempo at 10 bpm a time as you get better.

Practice single, double, an/or triple tounging 16 notes at a slow tempo. Then increase tempo at 10 bpm a time as you get better.

practice subdiving a quarter not into three parts, then do the same with a half note into three parts and a whole note into three parts.

Get out a pencil and mark the phrase of music you are struggling with rhythmically. Draw lines on notes that fall on the beat get a metronome and practice clapping and counting the syncopated rhythm outloud.

split the count into smaller pieces. for example, if the song is in 7/4, split a measure into a set of three, then a set of 4 (or vice versa, depends on the song). Use this to get used to the feel of a uneven time signature

TIME FOR SOME RANGE TRAINING. This is a slow and patient process, sometimes it will take weaks to increase your range by one note. The most important thing is practicing so that you have 100% control over the new higher note you are trying to play. This means, you have to practice intervals once you start hiting the higher not you are trying to play. To get there, start by playing chromatically up to your new highest note.

play down chromatically to your lowest note. Be sure to support your sound with a lot of air. Get out a tuner to make sure you're playing in tune and you are playing the note you want to play

You must build up to controlling these intervals. Practicing go up or down between notes starting with a third, then a 5th,6th,7th,8th and so on. GO up or down in steps.

Mark your music so that you remember to play a certain dynamic, then practice playing that dynamic. In fact, practicing playing quietly is a great way to improve control over your instrument and tone quality

Get a metronome and practice your song under tempo but focusing on the articulation. First start by articulating a measure at a time without changin pitches. Then play the music as written and slowly increase tempo the more consistent you get.

LONG TONES ARE ESSENTIAL. Play long tones and try to keep your pitch as steady as possible. Also, practice playing quietly but with a good supportive sound

warm up with long tones with a drone. Practice listening and trying to have your sound align with the drone so there aren't any waves. make mental notes about which pitches normally are out of tune for you.
